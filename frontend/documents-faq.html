Dealer Documents & FAQ

Export documents provided with dealer orders, and answers to common B2B sourcing questions.

Shipping Documents

After the vehicle is loaded onto the vessel, we send the following export documents by email. Original documents are also dispatched by EMS or DHL for importer clearance.

📄

Original Bill of Lading

The primary shipping document proving ownership and authorising release of the vehicle at the destination port.

📋

Commercial Invoice

Our official invoice showing the vehicle details, transaction value, and trade terms (FOB/C&F/CIF).

🏭

Export Certificate

Temporary Export De-registration Certificate issued by Japanese authorities confirming the vehicle has been legally exported.

📌 Note

Additional documents may be required depending on the destination country's import regulations. Dealers and importers should confirm all local customs, duty, inspection, and registration requirements before ordering.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are the vehicles in the catalog in stock?

No. All vehicles shown in our catalog are past transaction examples. They are not currently in stock. The catalog is provided as a reference to help dealers understand price ranges, specifications, resale fit, and auction sourcing targets. Please submit a dealer RFQ for similar units.

Are all vehicles right-hand drive?

Yes. Gloria Trading specialises exclusively in Right-Hand Drive (RHD) vehicles. All vehicles we export from Japan are RHD. Dealers and importers must confirm that RHD vehicles are legal and commercially suitable in the resale market before ordering.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept payment by Telegraphic Transfer (T/T) only. We can accept payment in USD or Japanese Yen. Please send a copy of your T/T payment form by email after remittance.

Can I cancel my order?

Orders cannot be cancelled after we have purchased the vehicle from auction on your behalf. Please confirm model, budget, resale suitability, import rules, trade term, and deposit approval before authorizing purchase.

What is the difference between FOB, C&F, and CIF?

FOB: Vehicle cost, inland transport to the Japanese port, export customs clearance, and port-related handling charges in Japan. Ocean freight and marine insurance are not included.
C&F: FOB + ocean freight to the destination port.
CIF: FOB + ocean freight + marine insurance.

Can you quote for my destination port?

We regularly quote and ship to Ghana (Tema), Nigeria (Lagos), Benin (Cotonou), and Côte d’Ivoire (Abidjan). We can also check other ports. Please contact us with your destination port and preferred trade term for a freight quote.

How long does shipping take?

Shipping times vary depending on the destination port and vessel availability. Typically, transit from Japan to West African ports takes approximately 4 to 6 weeks. We will provide estimated arrival dates after vessel booking.

Is insurance available?

Yes. Marine insurance is available at an extra charge, especially for CIF quotes or when requested by the importer. Coverage is valid until the vehicle reaches the destination port and generally covers total loss only. Please request insurance at the time of ordering.

How do I know the vehicle condition?

Before purchase, available auction sheets, grade, mileage, repair history, and photos should be checked according to the order status. After purchase, we send available photos and condition information for confirmation before requesting the balance payment.

What if my preferred models are not in the catalog?

If your preferred resale models are not listed in our reference catalog, please contact us or submit a Dealer RFQ. We can search Japanese auctions for model lists such as Corolla, Vitz, Probox, Hiace, Aqua, Fit, and other repeat-order targets.

Need Documents or Dealer Sourcing Details?
